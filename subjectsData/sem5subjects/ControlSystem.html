  • 1. Step & Impulse Response of Second-Order Unity Feedback System

    Overview

    Analyzing the step and impulse response of a second-order unity feedback system provides insights into the stability and transient behavior of the system.

    Theoretical Concepts

    Key Parameters

    • Natural frequency (ωn)
    • Damping ratio (ζ)
    • Settling time
    • Peak time

    Formulas

    • Transfer Function: \( T(s) = \frac{\omega_n^2}{s^2 + 2ζω_ns + \omega_n^2} \)
    • Step Response: \( c(t) = 1 - e^{-ζω_nt} \)
    • Impulse Response: \( h(t) = ω_n e^{-ζω_nt} \sin(ω_d t) \)

    Viva Questions

    1. Define step and impulse response.

    Answer: Step response shows the system's reaction to a unit step input, while impulse response represents the output for a unit impulse input.

    2. How is the damping ratio related to system stability?

    Answer: A higher damping ratio indicates better stability with minimal oscillations.

    3. What is natural frequency?

    Answer: The frequency at which a system oscillates when not damped.

  • 4. Transfer Function of DC Machine

    Overview

    The transfer function of a DC machine relates its input voltage to the output speed or torque.

    Theoretical Concepts

    Key Parameters

    • Armature resistance and inductance
    • Back EMF constant
    • Torque constant

    Transfer Function

    • Speed: \( \frac{\omega(s)}{V_a(s)} = \frac{K_t}{(J_s + B)(L_a s + R_a) + K_b K_t} \)

    Viva Questions

    1. Define transfer function.

    Answer: The mathematical representation of the relationship between input and output.

    2. What is back EMF?

    Answer: The voltage induced in the armature winding opposing the applied voltage.

  • 5. Unit Step Response Analysis

    Overview

    Plotting the unit step response of a transfer function provides insights into the dynamic behavior and system stability.

    Theoretical Concepts

    Key Parameters

    • Delay Time (td)
    • Rise Time (tr)
    • Peak Time (tp)
    • Peak Overshoot (Mp)

    Formulas

    • Transfer Function: \( G(s) = \frac{K}{s^2 + 2ζω_n s + \omega_n^2} \)
    • Response: \( C(t) = 1 - e^{-ζω_n t} [\cos(ω_d t) + (ζ/√(1-ζ^2)) \sin(ω_d t)] \)

    Viva Questions

    1. What is the significance of rise time?

    Answer: It is the time taken for the system output to rise from 10% to 90% of its final value.

    2. How do you measure peak overshoot?

    Answer: It is the maximum deviation above the steady-state value, usually expressed as a percentage.

  • 6. Pole-Zero Configuration in S-Plane

    Overview

    The location of poles and zeros in the s-plane determines the stability and response of the system.

    Theoretical Concepts

    Key Definitions

    • Pole: The value of \( s \) where the transfer function goes to infinity.
    • Zero: The value of \( s \) where the transfer function becomes zero.

    Viva Questions

    1. What is the significance of poles in the s-plane?

    Answer: Poles determine system stability; all poles should be in the left half-plane for stability.

    2. What does a zero on the s-plane indicate?

    Answer: Zeros affect the system response but not its stability.

  • 9. P, PI, PID Controllers for Second-Order Systems

    Overview

    Proportional, Integral, and Derivative (PID) controllers improve the performance of control systems by addressing error, rate of change, and accumulated error.

    Theoretical Concepts

    Controller Functions

    • P Controller: Corrects proportional to error.
    • PI Controller: Adds integral action to eliminate steady-state error.
    • PID Controller: Combines P, I, and D actions for optimal performance.

    Viva Questions

    1. What are the advantages of a PID controller?

    Answer: Provides fast response, eliminates steady-state error, and improves stability.

    2. What is the main drawback of a PI controller?

    Answer: It may result in overshoot due to accumulated error correction.
